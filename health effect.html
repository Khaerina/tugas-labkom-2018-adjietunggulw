Acute Effects
Contact with 2-hexanone can irritate the skin and eyes.
Inhalation can cause coughing and wheezing.
Exposure can cause headache, dizziness, light-headedness, and fainting.
Chronic Effects
Prolonged inhalation of 2-hexanone can harm the nervous system.
Workers who were exposed to 2-hexanone in the air for almost a year felt weakness, numbness, and tingling in the skin of the hands and feet.
Chronic exposure can also cause drying and cracking of the skin with redness and rash.
Reproductive/Development Effects
2-hexanone damaged the male (testes) reproductive system
Pregnant rats inhaled it did not gain as much weight during their pregnancy, had fewer babies, and had babies that were smaller and less active than the rats that were not exposed.
2-hexanone affects human reproduction or causes birth defects.
Carcinogenicity
The Department of Health and Human Services has not classified 2-hexanone as to human carcinogenicity. In addition, the International Agency for Research on Cancer and the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) have not classified 2-hexanone as to human carcinogenicity. There is no information available on the potential carcinogenic effects of 2-hexanone in people or in experimental animals.
